Project Overview

This project involved the complete electrical design of a commercial building consisting of a ground floor, two upper floors, and a roof. The system design adhered strictly to the Egyptian code, ensuring proper lighting distribution, safety, and load management.

My Role

My personal contribution to the team project was handling the entire lighting design process. This included the initial lighting simulation using DIALux Evo, laying out the lighting on AutoCAD, connecting lighting circuits, preparing panel schedules specific to lighting, and creating the final shop drawings. I also classified lighting loads into Normal and Emergency categories.

Lighting Design

Lighting fixtures were selected based on room usage and classified into Normal and Emergency loads. DIALux Evo software was used to perform accurate lighting simulations, ensuring proper illumination levels in all areas and full compliance with the Egyptian Electrical Code.

Types of Fixtures Used:

  • 60x60 Surface Mounted
  • Circular LED (IP20/IP44)
  • Ceiling Mounted Linear LED Profile (IP44)
  • Wall Mounted Circular
  • Magnetic Track
  • Pendant LED Linear (IP44)
  • Circular Suspended LED
  • Wall Emergency LED for Stairs (IP44)
  • Exit Sign

Lighting Distribution

After simulation, lighting was distributed and documented on AutoCAD. Each floor had a separate lighting panel, and circuits were connected appropriately. Lighting schedules were created, and circuit protection was applied based on Egyptian standards.

Tools Used

  • DIALux Evo for lighting simulation
  • AutoCAD for layout and shop drawings
