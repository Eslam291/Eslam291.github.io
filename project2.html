This project showcases the design and implementation of a fully automated color mixing system capable of producing up to 15 distinct final paint colors by mixing 7 base colors. It was developed as one of two major graduation projects, and it demonstrates real-world application of industrial automation, process control, and instrumentation.

üí° Project Concept

The system is designed to produce multiple paint colors based on predefined ratios stored in the PLC's internal database. Once a color is selected, the machine automatically mixes the required base colors in the correct quantities, and handles the entire filling and cleaning process autonomously.

‚öôÔ∏è How It Works

The machine can produce up to 15 different paint colors by mixing from 7 base colors, following a sequenced process:

  • Temperature Monitoring: A PT100 sensor measures the temperature inside the main tank to ensure conditions are suitable for mixing.
  • Color Dispensing with Flow Measurement: Each of the 7 base color tanks delivers liquid by gravity flow into the main tank. The amount of each color is precisely calculated using 8 Flow Meters, ensuring accurate mixing based on preprogrammed recipes.
  • Level Monitoring:
    • A Level Sensor inside the main tank verifies the volume received after each color is added.
    • An external Level Sensor near the packaging station checks the amount dispensed into the container, acting as a double-check mechanism during filling.
  • Filling Trigger: A Proximity Sensor confirms the presence of a packaging container before allowing filling to start.

üßº CIP System (Cleaning In Place)

After each mixing cycle, a Cleaning in Place (CIP) process is triggered to clean the main tank and its pipelines. This is handled via 4 dedicated tanks:

  • Water
  • Drain
  • Caustic
  • Acid

Each tank is equipped with:

  • A Pump
  • A Valve (with an integrated Proximity Sensor to confirm full valve opening before the pump starts)

An extra Drain Pump is used to empty the main tank at the end of each cleaning cycle.

üîß Components Used

  • PLC: Siemens S7-315 2PN/DP
  • HMI: Siemens TP900
  • Temperature Sensor: PT100
  • Level Sensors: 2 (one inside the main tank, one for filling validation)
  • Flow Meters: 8 (for precise color dosing)
  • Proximity Sensors: For bottle detection and valve position confirmation
  • Valves: Equipped with proximity feedback
  • Pumps: For fluid movement in CIP and drain systems
