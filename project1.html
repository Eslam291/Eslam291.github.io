Packing and Detect Defective Product Machine

Overview

This project involves the design and implementation of an automated machine that detects defective products through a multi-stage inspection process. The system ensures that only products meeting the required quality standards proceed to packaging and labeling.

Process Breakdown

  • 1. Packaging Stage (Mechanism not fully drawn): The product box is first guided into a packaging station. Once a sensor detects the box at a specific position, a pneumatic piston is activated to temporarily hold the box in place. This allows the fourth side of the box to be closed. The other three sides are mechanically closed using fixed guides. While the packaging mechanism was partially conceptualized.
  • 2. Size Detection Stage: This is the first actual defect detection stage. A pneumatic piston and two sensors are installed around a gate. If the first sensor detects a box but the second one doesn't receive a signal within a specific time, it indicates that the box is oversized and stuck at the gate. In this case, the piston ejects the defective product.
  • 3. Metal Content Inspection: A metal detector is integrated into the conveyor to detect the amount of metal inside the product. The system works by measuring the frequency change as the product passes through the machine. A capacitive proximity sensor was intentionally avoided here, as it cannot detect hidden or internal metallic objects inside the box. This stage helps identify foreign metallic objects like forgotten nuts or spoons, which should not be present. Based on the metal level, the system decides whether the product should be rejected or passed to the next stage.
  • 4. Weight Check via Load Cell: On a second conveyor, a load cell is used to verify that the product weight falls within an acceptable range. Products that fail this test are considered defective and removed.
  • 5. Printing and Acceptance Stage: If a product passes all previous stages, it reaches the printing station where a proximity sensor detects the product, and a piston activates a printing device to label the box. It is then directed to the "Accepted" collection area.

Note: A Delta VFD (Variable Frequency Drive) was also used to control the conveyor motor speed, ensuring smooth and synchronized movement that doesn’t interfere with piston timing.

Technical Highlights

  • Sensors Used: Proximity sensors, Load cell, Metal detection unit
  • Actuators: Pneumatic pistons for controlling flow and rejection
  • Control System: Siemens S7-313 PLC, Siemens TP900 HMI, Two conveyors (main + inspection)
  • Drive: Delta VFD for motor speed control

My Role in the Project

I was heavily involved in programming the programmable logic controller (PLC), setting the logic for some inspection stages and controlling the acceptance and rejection processes. I also participated in developing the HMI interface for monitoring sensor status and controlling piston movement in real time.
